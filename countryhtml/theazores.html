About The Azores

Europe's hidden gem in the middle of the Atlantic

The Azores archipelago consists of nine volcanic islands situated in the North Atlantic Ocean, about 1,500 kilometers west of mainland Portugal. This autonomous region is renowned for its stunning natural beauty, sustainable tourism practices, and rich cultural heritage.

Often described as Europe's Hawaii, The Azores feature dramatic landscapes with crater lakes, hot springs, lush forests, and rolling green pastures that reach all the way to the sea. The islands are surrounded by crystal-clear waters teeming with marine life, making them a paradise for whale watching and diving enthusiasts.

Each of the nine islands—São Miguel, Santa Maria, Terceira, Faial, Pico, São Jorge, Graciosa, Flores, and Corvo—has its own unique character and attractions, offering visitors diverse experiences in one destination.

Azores Highlights

Must-see destinations and experiences

Sete Cidades

Marvel at the famous twin lakes—one blue, one green—nestled in a massive volcanic crater on São Miguel Island. The Vista do Rei viewpoint offers the most iconic panorama in the Azores.

Mount Pico

Climb Portugal's highest mountain (2,351m) on Pico Island for breathtaking views of the archipelago. The challenging hike rewards adventurers with an unforgettable sunrise or sunset experience.

Furnas Valley

Experience the geothermal wonders of São Miguel, including hot springs, bubbling mud pools, and the traditional cozido stew cooked underground by volcanic heat.

Whale Watching

Spot over 20 species of cetaceans in the waters surrounding the islands. The Azores is one of the world's best destinations for whale and dolphin watching year-round.

Terceira Island

Explore the UNESCO World Heritage city of Angra do Heroísmo, with its colorful historical center, and discover Algar do Carvão, an ancient volcanic chimney you can walk inside.

Flores Island

Visit the westernmost point of Europe, known for its stunning waterfalls, deep ravines, and serene lakes. Flores is a paradise for hikers and nature photographers.

Best Time to Visit

Plan your Azorean adventure wisely

Summer (June-August): Peak season with warm temperatures (20-25°C), lowest rainfall, and calmest seas. Perfect for swimming, hiking, and whale watching, but expect more tourists and higher prices.

Spring (April-May): The islands burst into bloom with colorful hydrangeas and other flowers. Mild temperatures and fewer crowds make this an excellent time for hiking and sightseeing.

Fall (September-October): Similar to spring with mild weather, fewer tourists, and lower prices. The ocean remains warm enough for swimming and water activities.

Winter (November-March): The mild Atlantic climate means winters are rarely cold (14-18°C), but expect more rain and wind. Great for budget travelers and those seeking solitude, though some attractions may have limited hours.

Remember that weather in the Azores can be unpredictable year-round – locals often say you can experience four seasons in one day!
