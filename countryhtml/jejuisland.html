About Jeju Island

South Korea's natural wonder and UNESCO World Heritage site

Jeju Island, known as the "Island of the Gods," is South Korea's largest island and most popular holiday destination. Located in the Korea Strait, this volcanic island boasts stunning natural landscapes that earned it recognition as one of the New 7 Wonders of Nature.

Dominated by Mount Hallasan, South Korea's highest peak at 1,950 meters, Jeju features dramatic coastal cliffs, lava tubes, waterfalls, and pristine beaches. The island's unique ecosystem has been recognized with multiple UNESCO designations, including World Natural Heritage, Global Geopark, and Biosphere Reserve.

Beyond its natural beauty, Jeju offers a distinct culture with its own dialect, cuisine, and traditions. The island is famous for its iconic haenyeo (female free divers), tangerine orchards, and stone grandfather statues called dol hareubang that serve as guardians and symbols of the island.

Jeju Island Highlights

Must-see destinations and experiences

Mount Hallasan

Hike South Korea's highest mountain and explore its massive crater lake. Several trails of varying difficulty offer spectacular views of the island and unique alpine vegetation.

Seongsan Ilchulbong

Also known as "Sunrise Peak," this dramatic tuff cone crater rising from the ocean offers one of Korea's most spectacular sunrise views after a short but steep hike.

Manjanggul Cave

Explore one of the world's longest lava tubes with unique formations including the massive lava column. The cave maintains a cool temperature year-round, providing relief during summer.

Jeongbang Waterfall

Experience the rare sight of a waterfall that plunges directly into the ocean. This 23-meter cascade is surrounded by lush vegetation and black volcanic rocks.

Udo Island

Take a short ferry ride to this charming island with white sand beaches, black volcanic formations, and scenic lighthouses. Rent a bicycle or scooter to explore its coastal roads.

Haenyeo Demonstration

Witness Jeju's legendary female free divers in action as they harvest seafood without breathing equipment. This unique cultural tradition has been recognized by UNESCO.

Best Time to Visit

Plan your Jeju adventure wisely

Spring (April-May): The island bursts with cherry blossoms and yellow canola flowers. Pleasant temperatures (15-20°C) make this an ideal time for hiking and outdoor activities.

Summer (June-August): Peak tourist season with warm weather (25-30°C) perfect for beach activities and swimming. Expect crowds, higher prices, and occasional typhoons in late summer.

Fall (September-November): Perhaps the most beautiful season as the island transforms with autumn colors. Clear skies and mild temperatures (15-25°C) make it ideal for hiking Mount Hallasan.

Winter (December-March): The least crowded season, offering unique experiences like snow on Mount Hallasan contrasting with evergreen tangerine orchards. Temperatures are mild (5-10°C) compared to mainland Korea.

Jeju's subtropical climate means it has more rainfall than the Korean mainland, particularly during the summer monsoon season in July and August. The island is also prone to strong winds year-round, earning it the nickname "Island of the Three Abundances": wind, stones, and women.
